Evidence Quality

Primary vs secondary sources
Data reliability and methodology
Statistical rigor where applicable



Logical Reasoning

Valid vs fallacious reasoning
Appropriate confidence in conclusions



Presentation Balance

Acknowledgment of counterarguments
Fair representation of uncertainty
Avoidance of emotional manipulation



Epistemological Integrity

Consistent evidentiary standards
Appropriate skepticism applied uniformly
Avoidance of special pleading



Transparency

Clear methodology disclosure
Accessible primary sources
Honest about limitations



Limitation Acknowledgment

Recognition of what cannot be determined
Explicit uncertainty factors
Appropriate epistemic humility
